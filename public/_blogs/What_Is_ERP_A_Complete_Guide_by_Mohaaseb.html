What Is ERP? A Complete Guide by Mohaaseb

ERP stands for Enterprise Resource Planning — software that connects inventory, purchasing, sales, and finance into one system so every department works from the same real-time data. If you've ever managed stock in one spreadsheet and accounting in another, you already know the problem ERP solves.

Core Modules of an ERP System

Inventory

Track stock across every branch and warehouse.

Purchasing

Manage suppliers and reorder automatically.

Sales

Connect orders — including from a POS — directly to stock.

Finance

General ledger updated as transactions happen.

ERP vs POS: Where Does It Fit?

A common point of confusion is the difference between ERP and POS. A POS handles the checkout moment; ERP manages everything that happens before and after — restocking, supplier orders, multi-branch visibility, and financial reporting. They're complementary, not competing.
