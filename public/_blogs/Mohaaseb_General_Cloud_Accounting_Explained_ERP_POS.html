General cloud accounting — known in Arabic as الحسابات العامة السحابية — is the foundation that ties every transaction in a business back to a single, accurate set of books. Instead of keeping ledgers on a local machine or in scattered spreadsheets, general cloud accounting keeps your chart of accounts, journal entries, and financial statements accessible anywhere, updated the moment something happens at the register or in the warehouse.

What Is General Cloud Accounting (حسابات عامة سحابي)?

General cloud accounting refers to a centralized, always-online accounting engine that records every debit and credit generated across your business — sales, purchases, payroll, and adjustments — inside one cloud-based general ledger. For a محاسب (accountant), this means no more importing files or reconciling exports; the numbers are already there, correct and current.

Why Every ERP and POS System Needs a Cloud General Ledger

An ERP system plans and manages resources, and a POS system processes sales at the counter — but without a shared general ledger, the two can drift apart. Cloud general accounting is the glue: it makes sure a sale rung up on the POS terminal is instantly reflected in the ERP's financial reports, with zero manual entry.

  • Single source of truth for every account balance across branches.
  • Automatic journal entries generated from POS and ERP transactions.
  • Real-time financial statements — income statement, balance sheet, and cash flow — always current.
  • Multi-user access so your accountant, manager, and auditor see the same live numbers.
Tip: Businesses that move their general accounting to the cloud typically close their monthly books in a fraction of the time it used to take with spreadsheets and manual imports.
