5 Smart Ways Mohaaseb's POS and ERP Tools Help You Manage Purchases and Stock

Combining a POS and an ERP in one platform sounds simple, but the real value shows up in the small, everyday decisions it improves. Here are five concrete ways Mohaaseb helps you manage purchases and stock better than running separate tools.

1. Reorder before you run out, not after

Mohaaseb watches sales velocity through the POS and compares it against current stock levels in the ERP. When an item is projected to run out before the next scheduled delivery, it flags a reorder — turning purchasing from a reactive scramble into a predictable routine.

2. One stock number, every branch, every channel

In-store POS

Every counter sale updates the same central stock ledger instantly.

Multi-branch ERP

Transfers between locations are logged and reflected everywhere at once.

Purchasing

Incoming stock from suppliers appears the moment it's received, not when someone types it in later.

3. Purchase costs land straight in your accounts

Because Mohaaseb is محاسب — an accountant — at its core, every purchase order you approve automatically creates the matching accounting entry. There's no separate step to "sync" purchasing with bookkeeping; it's the same transaction viewed from two angles.

4. Spot slow-moving and fast-moving stock instantly

Mohaaseb's reporting layer, fed by both ERP purchase data and POS sales data, ranks products by turnover. That means you can adjust future purchase orders based on what's actually selling, not what you assumed would sell six months ago.

5. Keep suppliers and purchase history in one place

  • Every supplier record includes past orders, pricing history, and lead times.
  • Purchase orders link directly to the stock items they replenish.
  • Receiving discrepancies are flagged automatically against the original order.

Bottom line: when your POS, purchasing, and accounting share one system, managing purchases and stock stops being a monthly chore and becomes a live, always-accurate part of running the business.
