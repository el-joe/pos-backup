Businesses often start with a simple POS for daily sales and later bolt on an ERP for accounting and purchasing. The result is two systems that don't talk to each other. mohaaseb was built to remove that gap entirely, giving businesses one platform for selling, buying, and accounting.

The Old Way: Separate ERP and POS Tools

With disconnected systems, every sale made at the counter has to be re-entered, exported, or reconciled inside the accounting software. This creates delays, double data entry, and mismatches between what the shop floor sees and what the finance team reports.

What the ERP Side Handles

  • Accounting: general ledger, chart of accounts, journal entries, and financial statements.
  • Purchasing: purchase orders, supplier management, and cost tracking.
  • Inventory: multi-branch stock levels, transfers, and valuation.
  • Reporting: profit and loss, cash flow, and management dashboards.

What the POS Side Handles

  • Checkout: fast sales entry, discounts, and multiple payment methods.
  • Receipts and returns: instant printing and easy exchange handling.
  • Shift management: cashier sessions, cash drawer reconciliation.
  • Customer records: loyalty and purchase history at the point of sale.

The Mohaaseb Difference

In mohaaseb, every POS transaction automatically becomes an ERP record: the accounting entry is posted, inventory is deducted, and reports update — with zero manual syncing. This is what makes mohaaseb.com different from stitching two separate products together.

Who Should Switch to a Unified ERP POS System

Retail chains, restaurants, pharmacies, and distributors that need both fast checkout and accurate books benefit most. If your team is still exporting POS sales into spreadsheets for the accountant, mohaaseb.com can remove that step permanently.
