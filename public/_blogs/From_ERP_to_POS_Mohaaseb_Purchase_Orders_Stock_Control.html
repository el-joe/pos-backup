From ERP to POS: How Mohaaseb.com Simplifies Purchase Orders and Stock Control

A purchase order is only useful if it eventually connects to what actually happens at the register. That connection — from the back-office ERP where purchasing decisions are made, to the front-line POS where stock leaves the shelf — is where most business software falls apart. Mohaaseb, available at mohaaseb.com, was built specifically to keep that chain unbroken.

The problem with disconnected purchase orders

In a typical setup, a manager raises a purchase order in one tool, receives goods into a separate inventory sheet, and the cashier's POS system has no idea any of this happened until someone manually updates stock counts — often days later. By then, the numbers on screen rarely match what's actually on the shelf. Mohaaseb removes that gap by running purchasing, receiving, and point-of-sale on one shared ledger.

How Mohaaseb takes you from order to shelf

  • Create the purchase order inside Mohaaseb's ERP module, selecting the supplier, items, and expected delivery date.
  • Receive stock against that order — quantities and costs update your inventory and accounting automatically.
  • Sell through the POS — every sale immediately deducts from the same stock figures the ERP just updated.
  • Reconcile automatically — Mohaaseb compares expected vs. received vs. sold quantities so discrepancies surface instantly instead of at month-end.

Why it matters: when purchase orders, stock levels, and POS sales all live in the same system, you can trust the numbers on your dashboard the moment you look at them — no waiting for an end-of-day reconciliation.

Stock control that scales with your business

Whether you're managing one storefront or a network of branches, Mohaaseb's ERP core gives you a single view of stock across every location, while each branch's POS keeps operating fast and independently. Transfers between branches, partial deliveries, and supplier returns are all tracked against the original purchase order, so nothing falls through the cracks.
