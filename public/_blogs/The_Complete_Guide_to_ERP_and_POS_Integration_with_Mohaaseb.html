ERP and POS Integration: The Fundamentals

ERP (Enterprise Resource Planning) covers the operational backbone of a business — purchasing, inventory, accounting, and reporting. POS (Point of Sale) is where the customer-facing transaction happens. ERP and POS integration is the practice of tying these together so that every sale updates the ERP instantly, and every ERP-side decision — pricing, promotions, stock levels — is reflected at the register in real time.

Done well, integration turns the POS from a simple cash register into a live data source that feeds the entire business.

Key Workflows an Integrated ERP and POS Enable

Sell-Through Visibility

Managers see which products are moving fastest across every branch, without waiting for a weekly report.

Automated Reordering

When stock at the POS hits a threshold, the ERP's purchasing module can flag or trigger a reorder automatically.

Accurate Cost of Goods Sold

Because POS sales and ERP inventory valuation share the same data, gross margin figures are always current.

Consistent Pricing

Price changes made in the ERP apply instantly at every POS terminal — no manual updates per branch.
