Manage Purchases and Stock with Mohaaseb: The ERP and POS System Built for Growing Businesses

Every growing business eventually hits the same wall: purchase orders live in one spreadsheet, stock counts live in another, and the POS at the front counter has no idea what either of them say. Mohaaseb was built to close that gap. As a combined ERP and POS system, Mohaaseb lets you manage purchases and stock from a single dashboard, so every sale, delivery, and reorder updates the same source of truth in real time.

Why "manage purchases and stock" is the phrase that matters most

Ask any shop owner what keeps them up at night and it usually comes down to two things: running out of a best-seller, or overordering something that sits on a shelf for months. Both problems trace back to the same root cause — purchasing decisions made without accurate stock data. Mohaaseb's ERP core tracks supplier orders, expected delivery dates, and landed cost, while the POS layer records every unit sold the moment it happens. The result is a purchasing workflow that reacts to real demand instead of guesswork.

  • Automatic reorder points: Mohaaseb flags items approaching their minimum stock level and can generate a draft purchase order automatically.
  • Supplier tracking: Every purchase is linked to a vendor record, so you always know lead times and pricing history.
  • Real-time stock sync: A sale rung up on the POS instantly decrements available stock across every connected branch.
  • Cost visibility: Purchase costs flow directly into your accounting ledger — no manual re-entry required.

How the ERP and POS layers work together

In most software, ERP and POS are two separate products stitched together with an integration that breaks the moment something changes. Mohaaseb was designed the other way around: one database, one system, two views. The back office sees the full ERP picture — purchasing, accounting, multi-branch inventory — while the cashier sees a fast, simple POS screen. Because they share the same data, there is no sync delay and no risk of the two disagreeing about what's in stock.
