The ERP vs. POS Question Every Growing Business Faces

A POS system is what your cashier uses to ring up a sale. An ERP system is what your finance team uses to close the books, track assets, and plan purchasing. Traditionally these were separate products from separate vendors, requiring manual exports, imports, and reconciliation between them.

What Each System Does on Its Own

  • POS: Sales processing, payment collection, receipts, and basic stock deduction at checkout.
  • ERP: Accounting, purchasing, multi-branch inventory management, HR, and financial reporting.

Used separately, a business must manually sync data between its POS and ERP — a process that's slow and error-prone, especially as sales volume grows.

Why Mohaaseb Merges ERP and POS Into One Platform

mohaaseb.com was designed so that every sale at the POS is automatically an ERP transaction: it updates inventory, generates the accounting entry, and feeds your reports — with nothing to export or re-enter.
